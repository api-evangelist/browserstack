The hard part now isn&apos;t adoption. It&apos;s the messy middle: integration problems, test suites that were supposed to get faster but somehow got more complicated, and the quiet uncertainty about what all of this means for your team long-term.</p><p><a href="https://www.browserstack.com/events/breakpoint-2026?utm_source=company-blog&amp;utm_medium=PR&amp;utm_platform=&amp;utm_content=digitalevent&amp;utm_campaign=Summit%20Breakpoint-12-May-2026-Testing-Reimagined-by-Intelligent-AI&amp;utm_campaigncode=701OW00000fkoF8YAI&amp;utm_term=breakpoint_blog" rel="noreferrer">Breakpoint 2026</a> (12&#x2013;15 May 2026, virtual, free) is built around exactly that moment: after the pilot, before everything clicks.</p><h3 id="what-is-breakpoint"><a href="https://www.browserstack.com/events/breakpoint-2026?utm_source=company-blog&amp;utm_medium=PR&amp;utm_platform=&amp;utm_content=digitalevent&amp;utm_campaign=Summit%20Breakpoint-12-May-2026-Testing-Reimagined-by-Intelligent-AI&amp;utm_campaigncode=701OW00000fkoF8YAI&amp;utm_term=breakpoint_blog" rel="noreferrer">What is Breakpoint?</a></h3><p>Breakpoint is BrowserStack&apos;s annual virtual conference for the global software testing community. It started in 2020 and has run every couple of years since, growing to 20,000+ attendees by 2025. It&apos;s always been free.</p><p>The format hasn&apos;t changed much across editions: practitioner-led sessions, no fluff, real workflows from people who&apos;ve actually shipped the thing they&apos;re talking about. </p><p>This year&apos;s theme is <strong>Testing, Reimagined by Intelligent AI.</strong> Because most teams are somewhere between &quot;we&apos;ve adopted AI&quot; and &quot;we&apos;ve figured out AI,&quot; and that gap is where the interesting work is happening.</p>

<h3 id="some-of-the-people-on-stage-more-to-come">Some of the people on stage (more to come)</h3><p>The full speaker lineup is still coming together, but here are a few of the confirmed sessions:</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/juliodelimas/?ref=browserstack.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Julio de Lima</strong></a><strong> (QA Manager, Capco)</strong> is doing a live build. He&apos;ll write an API, design tests for it, automate them, and push them through a pipeline, all using AI code assistants like Copilot. The value isn&apos;t the tools, it&apos;s watching someone who actually does this daily show you how it fits together. If you&apos;ve been circling API test automation without knowing where to start, this one&apos;s for you.</li><li><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/brittanybstewart/?ref=browserstack.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Brittany Stewart</strong></a><strong> (Senior QA Specialist, QualityWorks Consulting Group)</strong> is presenting what she calls &quot;Centaur Mode.&quot; She&apos;s logged real numbers from her own practice: 75% less time creating test scenarios after working AI into her workflow. But the session isn&apos;t about the win. It&apos;s about everything that went wrong first, and the model she landed on where the human stays firmly in the loop. </li><li><a href="https://uk.linkedin.com/in/keithklain?ref=browserstack.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Keith Klain</strong></a><strong> (Director of Quality Engineering, KPMG)</strong> is asking out loud something a lot of people think privately: has test engineering actually delivered what it promised? His session is called &quot;The Death of Test Engineering&quot; and it&apos;s not a gentle take. If you&apos;ve ever sat through a testing ROI conversation that felt disconnected from reality, this will feel like fresh air.</li><li><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/lena-pejgan-nystrom/?ref=browserstack.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Lena Nystr&#xf6;m</strong></a><strong>, CEO of Test Scouts AB</strong>, is presenting on how to read what your system is actually telling you when something breaks. Error messages, validation failures, alerts. She&apos;s been building software since 1999 and the session is as practical as that experience suggests.</li><li><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/pramodkyadav/?ref=browserstack.com" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Pramod Yadav</strong></a><strong> (Test and Automation Specialist, Power Tester)</strong> went from idea to shipped SaaS MVP in a few days using AI agents, and then used those same agents to build quality gates so the thing didn&apos;t break the moment he added a feature. It&apos;s a first-person account of agentic testing in the wild, not a walkthrough of what the tools claim to do.</li></ul><p>Day 3 is a full run of lightning talks: 10-minute sessions from practitioners sharing what they tried, what they measured, and what actually changed. More sessions and speakers are being added, so check the <a href="https://www.browserstack.com/events/breakpoint-2026?ref=browserstack.com">event page</a> for the latest.</p><h3 id="is-this-for-you">Is this for you?</h3><p>Whether you&apos;re a QA engineer looking for technical depth, an engineering manager thinking about team structure in an AI-driven world, or a developer who cares about what happens after code ships, Breakpoint has content built for you.</p><p>Sessions span beginner-accessible to advanced practitioner level, and there are dedicated tracks for both hands-on practitioners and people in leadership roles.</p>
